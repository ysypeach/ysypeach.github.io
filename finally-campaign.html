How do you drive preference for Logitech ConferenceCams?

/  Project Scope: Positioning, Branding, and Digital Marketing
/  Client: Logitech
/  Duration: 2015-2016
/  Team: Angel Yu (Lead Designer), Tammy Duarte (Designer), Nelson Ho (Creative Director),
    Dev Team, Content Team

In 2015, Logitech went through a brand renewal with remarkable interest in a growing young audiences driven by personal goals and lifestyles. The challenge: how do you effectively engage with this audience while communicating the values Logitech has to offer in the business setting? As the lead designer on Logitech's global Finally Campaign, I helped to reposition and evolve Logitech's videoconferencing products with an outcome of 100,000 systems in sales–this makes Logitech the number-two player in the business at the time of this project.

Creating Communications with Impact

Our work initiated with positioning and strategy. Having to work with a set of primary and secondary audience, the team studied profiles and pain points. From these, we came up with audience-specific messaging and communications.

Planning the Customer Path

We identified goals and actions for different phases of the persona purchase journey. These helped to determine appropriate media and channels. To coordinate our various digital communications, we crafted potential paths a customer would go through to reach final purchase.

Testing and Optimizing for Impact

To optimize the performance of communications, we collected feedbacks using a variety of tools such as heat maps and A/B testing. We used heat maps to track visitor interactions of the landing page, and A/B testing to track a variety of factors, such as: If seasonality played a role in results? Which performs best: product or lifestyle imagery? What headlines resonated: pain-point or benefit based?

Executing the Campaign

To give form to the campaign, I worked with strategists and copy writers to deliver a master creative, which conveyed Logitech's value proposition and supported world wide deployment through identity guideline and sample applications. Additionally, I worked with the dev team to bring some of these communications to life–through eblasts, landing pages, social media, and a range of print and environmental graphics.
