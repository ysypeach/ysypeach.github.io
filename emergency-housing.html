How might we improve incoming students' experience to university?

/  Project scope: responsive web design
/  Client: Carnegie Mellon University
/  Duration: three and half weeks, 2017
/  Team: Angel Yu and Nila Banerjee

Starting at a university is one of the biggest transition many of us make in our lives. There are many places this can go wrong, and looking back, if only we can leverage the experience we've had to create a better transition for others. I set out to research and design a better future by looking at students' experience at Carnegie Mellon University.

The Design Process

Learning Students' Experience

We used directed storytelling to quickly learn about the students’ experience, from the time they learn of their acceptance through when they arrive on campus. Some of the starter phrases we used include: “Tell me about your trip to Carnegie Mellon”, “What did you do to prepare for school?” and “How did you travel to campus?”

Narrowing Our Scope

We used affinity diagram to synthesize the stories we’ve heard. Seeing the notes in clusters helped us visually grasp areas of issues and opportunities. We learnt that most students experienced some kind of stress with housing. We knew that tackling the entire journey was an impossible task, so we decided to focus on housing for the remainder of our project.

Modelling and Reframing

Using the Analysis-Synthesis Bridge Model, we created a journey map to synthesize the students' current experience to getting to Carnegie Mellon. An idealized experience is mapped out through a second journey map below.

Ideating Possible Futures

Using a "see-think-do" framework, we generated many different scenarios to brainstorm possible customer experiences. We aimed at putting ourselves in the students' shoes. At the end of the ideation session, we compared each scenario and picked one that generated the most value.

Planning the User Flow

When a student lands on the homepage, they are briefly introduced to the emergency housing service through descriptions and user stories. They are prompted to book a room, which directs them to the next page where they can browse available options. Once a room is selected, they can read more about it and book a reservation. The last page demonstrates a confirmation which more links to explore. The experience is optimized for mobile.
